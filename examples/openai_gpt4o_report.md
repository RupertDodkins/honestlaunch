## claim_01: missing_context

**Confidence:** high

**Original:** Our system currently catches 100% of meaningful deviations from the system voice based on our internal evaluations, which includes samples generated by other system voices, clips during which the model used a voice from the prompt as part of its completion, and an assortment of human samples.

**Stretch Score:** 40/100

**Why:** The claim is a direct quote from the GPT-4o System Card and is technically supported by internal test results showing a 1.0 recall. However, presenting this 100% figure in isolation is misleading because it omits critical operational caveats. First, this perfect recall is achieved at the expense of precision (0.95-0.96), creating a 4% to 5% false positive rate that results in unnecessary call disconnections and usability issues. Second, the evaluations were conducted on a clean, internal dataset and did not systematically account for real-world acoustic perturbations (such as background noise, echoes, or low-quality audio) under which OpenAI admits safety robustness decreases.

**Defensible rewrite:** In clean internal evaluations, our voice classifier achieved a 100% catch rate (1.0 recall) for tested voice deviations, though this is accompanied by a 4-5% false positive rate (0.95-0.96 precision) that impacts user experience, and the performance is subject to degraded robustness under real-world audio perturbations.
